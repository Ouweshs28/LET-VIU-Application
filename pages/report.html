Complete Report Writing Tips Joy Mbeseka Ahmed (M.Sc. BISM Student)

There are different styles and shapes of report writing, which all depends on both the supervisor’s requirements and topic of choice. Some reports contain few components while others contain all the major ones.

In this guideline, the complete requirements for a successful report writing is given.

Reports are simply assignments with clear and easy structures. However, many individuals are taken aback when asked to write reports. Here, a step by step breakdown will be given to clear any confusion.

So, here we go – let’s dive into the guide to the complete report writing tips:

  1. The Proposal

The proposal is usually a brief and separate document accompanying the report. This is the first document sent to the supervisor with details of what the report would be about. Also, the basic requirements needed to accomplish the report is stated here.

  1. The Title Page

Depending on the title page requirements from an organization or the supervisors, here are the four basic information that have to be on the title page of any report.

  • The title of the report
  • The name of the author and university that originated the report
  • The report completion date
  1. The Acknowledgement

In report writing, the acknowledgement contains gratitude to those who helped the writer in the process. It should be sincere and referred to every individual separately instead of generalizing it.

  1. The Abstract

The abstract communicates the scope and topics discussed in the paper to the readers. It is the summary of the contents in the paper. In order to write a good abstract, it is adviced to summarize the whole report starting from the introduction to the conclusion in a sentence. Also, it is recommended to always write the abstract at the end of the report.

  1. The Table of Content

The table of content reflects the report structure. The sections and subsections should be numbered and titled correctly and in a logical manner to assist the reader finds his or her through the report

  1. The List of Figures, Tables

All the figures, tables and illustrations must be numbered according to the chapter number within which they are positioned. If the report has more than six figures, illustration or tables, they should be listed in a separate page but if they are less than six, they can be listed in the table of contents. It is adviced to list them in the following order:

  • List of figures
  • List of tables
  • List of illustration
  1. The Introduction

A good introduction in any report should be brief but thorough discussing the context of the problem. It typically should be about 1½ to 2 pages long and contains the following:

  • The background
  • Problem definition
  • Research question(s)
  • The objectives and purpose of the report
  1. The Body

The body of a report usually is the main section including all the materials and facts necessary for understanding the problem area. It consists of the following:

  • Literature review (this must be critical examination of previous literatures in the subject area)
  • Methodology (includes the kind of methods used in conducting the research, kind of data gathered, and the ethical considerations taken in the process of the report)
  • Empirical Investigation or Result (summarizes the findings about the new information discovered, confirmed or invented in the course of the research and should be presented in a straight forward manner)
  • Analysis and Discussion (this gives a description of both the wrong and right procedures made during the research. It is adviced to always demonstrate how the wrong procedures can be corrected and how to improve the right ones)
  1. The Conclusion

The conclusion is the last section in report writing. It is written in a concise and brief manner, pointing out the relevant points. It is adviced not to introduce any new information rather it should be a smooth transition from the body to the conclusion

  1. The Recommendation

Here, suggestions or directions to how the examined problem is given in a clear way.

  1. The References

The reference includes the alphabetical list of all the sources of information used during the investigation. (Keep records of all the numerous sources of information once you begin writing as this reduces the tedious task of looking for lost information). Although there are different referencing styles however, the harvard referencing style is the most common one and this also, depends on the university.

  1. The Appendices

In the appendices, all information necessary for completing the research but would bog it in the body should be included here. This may consist of the tables or figures, details of experimental configuration e.t.c

I hope that you found these writing tips very useful! Although there are different types of report writing, the above tips are a starting point for you to begin writing any report.
